    • You know your password, but your user account is locked out and you want to unlock it.
    In any of those cases you may get back in to your user account by resetting your password here:

    Change your two-step verification settings?

    Your user account is protected by two-step verification, also known as Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
    You may change your existing authentication methods here:

    Setup two-step verification

    In case that something with two-factor authentication or app passwords is not working as expected, and you can still access your user account, you may do a complete reset of your settings and app passwords here:

    Reset two-step verification

    Use apps w/o support for two-step verification?

    Certain non-browser apps, such as the Apple native email client that uses Exchange Active Sync, currently do not support two-step verification. To circumvent that, you can create individual passwords for each app which can then be used in the app instead of your normal password.
    App passwords can be managed here:

    Manage app passwords

    In case that something with app passwords is not working as expected, and you can still access your user account, you may reset all app passwords here:

    Reset app passwords
